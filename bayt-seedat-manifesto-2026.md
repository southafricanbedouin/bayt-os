## Part One — Our Vision

*What does the Seedat family look like in 2035?*

It is 2035. Yahya is 21. Isa is 20. Linah is 17. Dana is 15.

The house is loud in the best way — debates at the dinner table, someone reciting Quran in the next room, a project being planned, an idea being argued over with love. Muhammad and Camilla sit at the head of a family that has outgrown what they imagined when they started.

Each child carries something distinct. **Yahya leads** — quietly, steadily, the kind of person others follow without being asked. **Isa builds** — his mind sees systems and solutions where others see problems. **Linah gives** — she has always known how to see people, and by 17 she is already changing rooms when she walks into them. **Dana creates** — she has an eye for beauty and a voice that will not be quieted, and she is only just beginning.

They are not perfect. They have struggled, failed, doubted, and returned. But they *return* — that is what the Seedat family does. They return to Allah, to each other, to the values written in this document on the night it all began.

Muhammad and Camilla have not just raised children. They have raised *people* — people with faith that is their own, not inherited by accident but chosen with conviction. People who know how to work, how to give, how to sit with difficulty without running from it.

The family name means something in 2035. Not because of wealth or titles — but because of **character**. The Seedat name is attached to people who do what they say, who give more than they take, who remember Allah in private as much as in public.

That is what we are building. Not a picture-perfect family. A *real* one — grounded, growing, and going somewhere.

## Part Three — Our Non-Negotiables

*The five lines we do not cross — regardless of mood, money, or circumstance.*

**01 — Salah Is Not Optional**
In this house, the five prayers are not a personal preference. They are the structure around which everything else is arranged. When life gets hard — and it will — salah is what we return to first. Not later. *First.*

**02 — We Do Not Humiliate Each Other**
Discipline happens. Disagreement happens. But no family member — child or parent — will be shamed, mocked, or spoken to in a way that degrades their dignity. Correction is given with love. Always.

**03 — We Tell the Truth**
Even when it is uncomfortable. Even when the truth makes us look bad. Honesty in this family is not a virtue — it is a baseline. A Seedat who lies to protect themselves is not protected. They are diminished.

**04 — We Show Up for Each Other**
When a family member is struggling, we do not leave them to figure it out alone. We ask. We sit with them. We carry what we can carry. This family does not abandon its own.

**05 — Sadaqah Is Non-Negotiable**
A percentage of everything we earn — coin, income, resource — goes out. Not when we feel generous. Automatically. We are not the final destination of what Allah gives us. We are a channel.

## Part Four — Our Family Values

*Five values, ranked — as the Seedat family lives them.*

**01 — TAQWA — God-Consciousness**
In this family, we act as if Allah is watching — because He is. This is not fear. It is the highest form of awareness.

**02 — SIDQ — Truthfulness**
We say what we mean and mean what we say. In this house, your word is your bond — to your siblings, to your parents, and to yourself.

**03 — ILMU — Knowledge & Growth**
We are a family that reads, asks, learns, and improves. Ignorance is not excused by comfort. We grow or we stagnate — and we choose growth.

**04 — KHIDMA — Service**
We are not in this family for ourselves. We serve each other, our community, and anyone Allah places in our path. The best of us is the one who is best to his family — and beyond.

**05 — SABR — Purposeful Patience**
This family does not quit when things are hard. We breathe, we make du'a, we take the next small step. Sabr is not passivity — it is disciplined endurance with your eyes on Allah.

## Part Five — The Sulh Protocol

*How the Seedat family resolves conflict. These are not suggestions. They are the agreed process.*

**01 — Stop**
No conflict is resolved in heat. Whoever is most activated takes space first. Ten minutes minimum. No last words on the way out.

**02 — Seek Truth, Not Victory**
Before reopening the conversation, each person asks privately: *"Where am I wrong in this?"* Not whether the other person is wrong. Where *I* am wrong. Come back with that answer ready.

**03 — Speak, Then Listen**
Each person speaks without interruption. The rule: you may not respond until you can accurately repeat what the other person said. If you cannot repeat it, you were not listening — you were waiting to talk.

**04 — Seek Repair, Not Punishment**
The goal of every conflict resolution is *relationship repair*, not being proven right. Ask: *"What do we need to do so we can move forward?"*

**05 — Close With Forgiveness**
Every resolved conflict ends with the words — said aloud: *"I forgive you."* And where applicable: *"I ask for your forgiveness."* These words are not weakness. They are the most powerful words in our household.

**06 — Escalate If Needed**
If two people cannot resolve it between themselves, Muhammad or Camilla mediates. If the conflict involves a parent, the other parent mediates. No one is above this process.

## Part Six — The Shura Principle

*How decisions are made in this family — drawn from the Quranic principle of Shura (42:38).*

**Every Voice Is Heard**
From Dana at six to Muhammad at the head of the table — everyone may speak. No idea is too small to raise. No person is too young to have an opinion worth considering.

**Every Decision Is Weighed**
Before a decision is made, the question is asked: *"Does this align with our values and our non-negotiables?"* If it does not — no vote can override it.

**Children Lead Where They Are Capable**
As the children grow, they are given increasing responsibility to lead decisions in their domain. A child who plans the holiday owns the holiday. This is how leaders are made.

**Muhammad Decides When the Family Cannot**
If the Shura cannot reach consensus, Muhammad makes the final call — and explains why. Not because his opinion outweighs others, but because a family needs a captain. The captain is accountable to Allah first.

**No Decision Made in Anger Stands**
Any decision made in emotional heat is suspended until it can be revisited calmly. This applies to parents as much as children.

## Part Seven — A Letter to Our Children

*From Muhammad and Camilla, to Yahya, Isa, Linah, and Dana.*
*To be read at the first Shura. To be opened again when each of you leaves home.*

---

To Our Children,

We wrote this on a Monday night in 2026, in Doha, while you were all still small enough to be surprised by things and old enough to start understanding them.

We want you to know why we built this. We built it because we watched the world become louder and more confusing every year, and we made a decision: our home would be different. Not perfect — we are not naive enough to promise you perfect. But *different*. Intentional. A place where the important things are named out loud so they don't get lost in the noise.

We built it because we believe that the most important thing we will ever do in our lives is raise you. Not the companies, not the projects, not the content or the careers. You. The four of you are the legacy. Everything else is secondary.

We built it *with* you — not *for* you — because we wanted you to know from the beginning that you are not passengers in this family. You are co-founders. Your voice matters here. Your ideas matter here. The things you care about are taken seriously in this house.

*Yahya — we see how you carry responsibility like it was made for you. Let that be a strength, not a burden. You do not have to hold everything alone.*

*Isa — we see the way your mind works, always looking for how things connect, always building something. Build with intention. Build for good.*

*Linah — we see your heart. We have always seen it. The world needs more people who feel things as deeply as you do. Don't let anyone tell you that's too much.*

*Dana — we see your fire. Hold onto it. The world will try to calm you down. Some of that is wisdom. But the fire itself — the part that makes you you — protect that.*

We will not always get this right. We will make mistakes as parents that we cannot yet foresee. When we do — and when you are old enough to name them — please know: we were doing our best with what we had, and we loved you completely, always.

*The best of us is the one who is best to his family. We are trying to be that. We hope you will be too.*

With all of our love,
**Muhammad and Camilla**
